Europe has become a patchwork of residency options for global citizens. The two most popular paths—the Golden Visa and the Digital Nomad Visa—cater to very different needs.

## The Golden Visa (Residency by Investment)

Golden Visas are designed for high-net-worth individuals who want the right to live in an EU country without necessarily residing there full-time.

**Key Benefits:**
- **Path to Citizenship**: Most programs offer a route to permanent residency or citizenship after 5-7 years.
- **Minimal Residency Requirement**: In countries like Portugal, you only need to spend 7 days a year in the country.
- **Investment Focus**: Your residency is tied to an asset, usually real estate or a venture fund.

## The Digital Nomad Visa

This is a newer category designed for remote workers and entrepreneurs who want to experience living in Europe while maintaining their international business.

**Key Differences:**
- **Lower Cost**: There is usually no investment requirement, only a minimum monthly income threshold.
- **Full-Time Residence**: Most nomad visas require you to live in the country for a significant portion of the year.
- **Temporary Nature**: These are often valid for 1-2 years and may not always lead to permanent residency.

## Which Should You Choose?

If you are looking for an "insurance policy" and a long-term asset, the Golden Visa is superior. If you want a lifestyle change and an immediate, low-cost move, the Digital Nomad Visa is an excellent entry point.
